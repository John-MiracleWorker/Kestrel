# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We pledge to make participation in Kestrel a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless
of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity
and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal
appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive,
and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people;
- respecting differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences;
- giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback;
- accepting responsibility, apologizing to those affected by mistakes, and learning from them;
- focusing on what is best for the community and the project.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- sexualized language or imagery and unwelcome sexual attention or advances;
- trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks;
- public or private harassment;
- publishing another person's private information without explicit permission;
- other conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards. They may remove,
edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with
this Code of Conduct, and will communicate moderation reasons when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces and when an individual is officially representing
the project in public spaces.

## Reporting and Enforcement

The project does not currently publish a dedicated private conduct-reporting mailbox. For behavior
on GitHub that violates GitHub policy, use
[GitHub's private abuse-reporting channel](https://support.github.com/contact/report-abuse). Do not
put sensitive conduct details in a public Kestrel issue. This section must be updated with a
maintainer-controlled private channel before the project can claim private project-level conduct
reporting.

Do not use a public issue to report conduct that contains private or sensitive information.
When maintainers receive a report through an appropriate private channel, enforcement decisions may
range from a private correction through a temporary or permanent ban, depending on impact, intent,
and repeated behavior.
